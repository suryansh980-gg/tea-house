Brewing

Four minutes of doing nothing.

Good leaves don't need ceremony, only patience. Here is the whole method.

Measure

One generous teaspoon — about 2.5 g — for every 200 ml of water. Whole leaf looks like a lot. It isn't.

Heat

Boiling water scalds delicate leaves. Let the kettle rest a minute for blacks, two for greens and whites.

Steep

Cover the cup and walk away for the time printed on the tin. Resist stirring; the leaves know their work.

Steep again

Every tea we sell takes a second infusion, and the whites take a third. Add thirty seconds each time.
